Frequently Asked Questions

Telling myself I should/must perform to a high standard causes me to try harder

Having high standards that motivate you to try harder is not the issue. The problem is what you tell yourself when you fail to meet those standards and what that evaluation does to your wellbeing. If you tell yourself you are a failure, or that you're worthless for not meeting your goal, and that judgment leads you to engage in unhelpful behaviours (eg. procrastination) or develop unhelpful negative emotions such as shame or depression, are your high standards really serving your best interests? It would be more helpful to practise self-acceptance when you don't meet your high standards and view yourself as an imperfect human being like anyone else.

Why should I work so hard to change my way of thinking when the problem is caused by others?

It is understandably frustrating when the problem you have is due to the actions of others that is not under your control. If you have tried your utmost to control the situation or influence them, then what other option is left? If you were unable to cope or accept their actions, what is the consequence of that for you? You can choose to disagree with their behaviour while at the same time accepting it as a unfortunate reality.

Expecting the worst to happen helps me be prepared for the future

There are a few points here worth considering:

  • How likely is the worst to happen?

  • What could you be doing instead of preparing for the worst case scenario?

  • Could you cope acceptably well if you were less than completely prepared for the worst?

I would be 'going easy on myself' if I were to unconditionally accept myself after making a mistake

Unconditionally accepting yourself does not mean you avoid criticising your mistakes altogether. It means you do not define yourself by your mistakes. You can acknowledge the negative impact of your action while simultaneously accepting yourself. If anything, being excessively critical of yourself is easier way out because it excessively simplifies how you view yourself.

I understand the ABC model and agree with the disputations to my unhelpful beliefs, but I still experience the unhelpful emotions

Ask yourself how strongly you believe in the disputations and the alternative beliefs. At the start, you might only have a weak sense of confidence in changing your unhelpful belief. If you wholeheartedly accepted the alternative belief as a new way of thinking, what would your life look like?
