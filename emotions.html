There are many words we can use to describe our emotions and several attempts have been made to categorise emotions to allow us to better study them.

The emotions we are interested in addressing in therapy tend to be unhelpful ones that cause us suffering and prevent us from reaching our goals.

The emotions described here are not a definitive list of all emotion words, and in some cases the words we commonly use to describe emotions are better understood as a combination of beliefs and emotions. Understanding the distinction and having the capability to accurate describe our inner emotional state is key to good mental health.

This is a list of unhelpful emotions that we typically focus on in REBT. Note that the absence of some of these unhelpful negative emotions is not positive emotion, but an unhelpful negative emotion. It would be unrealistic if we felt good about something bad happened to us.

A negative emotion can be helpful if it leads us to take meaningful action to resolve a problem.

Depression occurs if you experience some form of loss, failure or undeserved adversity in your life.

If you are feeling depressed, you:

''Unhelpful Behaviours'' ''Helpful Behaviours''
You use self-destructive means to stop feeling depressed (eg. alcohol, drugs) You use coping strategies that do not harm yourself or others
You stop engaging in your usual activities because you lack energy or do not feel motivated to do them You engage in some of your usual activities even if you do not feel motivated to do them
You withdraw from others You continue to interact with others
You push away attempts by others to comfort you You allow yourself to be comforted to help you express sadness and mourn your loss
You become overly dependent on and seek to cling to others You look to others for support but do not solely rely on them to feel better
''Unhelpful thoughts'' ''Helpful thoughts''
You only focus on negative aspects of the loss/failure You can recognise both positive and negative aspects of the loss
You believe you are unable to help yourself (helplessness) You believe you are able to help yourself
You ruminate about what caused the depression and its consequences You are able to disengage from rumination and focus on the present

This emotion occurs when we perceive there is a threat in the future.

I should know what I am doing.

Unhelpful Behaviours Helpful Behaviours
You avoid the threat You face up to the threat
You ward off the threat (eg. superstitious behaviour) You take constructive action to minimize risk of the threat
You find ways to distract yourself from the threat
   
Unhelpful thoughts Helpful thoughts
You overestimate the probability of the threat occurring You are realistic about the probability of the threat occurring
You underestimate your ability to cope with the threat You are realistic about your ability to cope with the threat
You do not create an even more negative threat in your mind

Anger is a common negative emotion that occurs when we think someone is trying to harm us.

My children should show me respect.

''Unhelpful Behaviours'' ''Helpful Behaviours''
You attack the other person physically and/or verbally You practise being assertive with the other person
You focus your anger on something else (person, animal or object) other than the other person You take appropriate action at the other person
You withdraw aggressively You leave an unsatisfactory situation nonaggressively after taking steps to deal with it
''Unhelpful thoughts'' ''Helpful thoughts''
You are unwilling to consider the viewpoints of others You are willing to consider the viewpoints of others
You see yourself as definitely right and others as definitely wrong You do not see yourself as definitely right and others as definitely wrong
You only see malicious intent in the actions of others You can view others' actions as unintentional

The emotion of hurt occurs when we experience a significant other criticising or rejecting us when we do not think we deserved to be treated that way.

I can't stand it if my partner criticises me.
Unhelpful Behaviours Helpful Behaviours
You stop communicating to the other person You communicate your feelings to the other person directly
You indirectly criticize or punish the other person You directly attempt to influence the other person to behave more fairly towards you
   
Unhelpful thoughts Helpful thoughts
You overestimate the unfairness of the other person's behaviour You are realistic about the fairness of the other person's behaviour and view it in context of the situation
You believe that the other person does not care about you You think the other person has acted badly but do not take it as evidence of lack of caring or indifference
You see yourself as alone, uncared for, and misunderstood You do not see yourself as alone, uncared for, or misunderstood
You expect the other person to take the initiative to mend the relationship You do not expect the other person to take the initiative

Shame occurs when we are reminded of our own wrongdoing, perceive ourselves as fallen short of an ideal, or have been ridiculed by others in public.

Example thoughts:

I shouldn't feel this way.

Unhelpful Behaviours Helpful Behaviours
You avoid being around others You continue to participate socially
You try to distract yourself from the shame by engaging in impulsive actions or attacking/blaming others You take responsibility for managing your own shame
   
Unhelpful thoughts Helpful thoughts
You overestimate the likelihood that others will notice your shortcoming You have a realistic appraisal of how likely others are to notice your shortcoming
You overestimate the amount of judgment you expect from others for knowing your shortcoming You have a realistic appraisal of how much others judge you for your shortcoming
You judge yourself as inferior, defective, or not good enough for having the shortcoming You can practise compassion towards yourself and accept yourself and your failures with compassion

Guilt occurs when you do or think something you believe is morally wrong, or you believe you have caused harm to others.

I should not upset others.

Unhelpful Behaviours Helpful Behaviours
You use unhelpful means to avoid the painful feeling of guilt You face up to the painful feeling of guilt
You beg for forgiveness from those you wronged You ask but do not beg for forgiveness from those you wronged
You find ways to make excuses for your guilty actions You do not make excuses but take appropriate responsibility for your guilt
You reject offers of forgiveness You accept offers of forgiveness
   
Unhelpful thoughts Helpful thoughts
You assume more responsibility for the guilty action than is warranted You assume an appropriate level of responsibility
You think you will receive retribution You do not think you will receive retribution
You do not view your guilty actions in the overall context of what happened You view your guilty actions in the overall context and take into account the responsibility of others for what happened

Envy is the emotion we experience when we view ourselves in competition with someone else who we deem to have more than us. It is commonly confused with jealousy, which differs from envy as jealousy always involve at least three parties -- an individual being jealous of a competitor's relationship to a loved one. Envy, on the other hand, is usually about comparing ourselves to someone who has something we do not, which does not necessarily refer to a relationship.

I should have what others have.
